Question 5¶

Four assumptions:

  1. Linearity

As shown by the graph above, it is fair to assume that the relationship between mean life expectancy and year is linear.

  1. Homoscedasticity

There is one is tricky, as this has reason to change over time. In this time window however this changing varaiance in residuals across years doesnt seem to be a large factor.

  1. Independence

It makes sense to asusme independace in life expectancy across years.

  1. Normality

With such a large sample size, it is fair to assume that Y is most likely normally distributed. Some skew might be in effect but most observations should be very close to normal.

By definition, if the line is actually the line of best fit (assuming linear model), around 50% of the residuals will have a negative value, and 50% will have a positive value. The sum of all the residuals should be 0. The shape should of the violins should be the same as the frequency of data points around a certain value should have the same frequency in its residuals.
